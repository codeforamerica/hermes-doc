The problem

The criminal justice system is complicated. When citizens fail to understand or comply with its orders they put themselves at risk of entering a cycle of trouble.

Most defendants see a judge for only a few minutes at a time and it’s easy to be confused by proceedings. People are discharged from custody with requirements for follow ups which, if not met, could result in additional jail time. Individuals who miss court dates often have warrants issued for their arrest, causing trouble for them and extra work for administrators.

Statistics

18% In 2012, defendants missed court 18% of the time.

104 On average, during a single day in June 2012, 104 bench warrants were issued for people who failed to appear for their court date.

112% In the second week of March 2013, the main Louisville Metro jail complex hosted 1,521 inmates, with a rated capacity of 1,353 beds.

Our goal

To build a system helping people resolve their cases by reminding them of upcoming court dates, providing helpful instructions, and explaining consequences of the choices being made.
